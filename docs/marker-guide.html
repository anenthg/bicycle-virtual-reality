Where each band goes

This is roughly what your laptop camera sees, looking at the front of the bike.

Why these three?

The three hues are spaced roughly evenly around the wheel, so the tracker can tell them apart even in dim light or slight blur. Just as important: green, blue and magenta don't appear on skin, wood, cardboard or most walls, so the background won't create false blobs — unlike red, orange or brown.

👍 Do

  • Use matte fluorescent tape or stickers — the brighter and more saturated, the better.
  • Make each band ~3–4 cm wide so it's a solid blob to the camera.
  • Put grips on the very ends of the bars, outside where the hands sit, so fingers never cover them.
  • Put the blue band where the camera sees it clearly and it doesn't turn with the bars.
  • Raise the laptop so it sees the bars roughly head-on.

👎 Avoid

  • Glossy / metallic tape — glare blows out to white and loses its color.
  • Red, orange, brown, skin tones — too close to hands, wood and warm lighting.
  • Two markers the same-ish color — keep green, magenta and blue distinct.
  • Tiny or narrow strips — thin tape becomes an unreliable dot at a distance.
  • A blue band on the stem/bars that rotate — it must be on the fixed frame.
💡 These are the app's default colors. During calibration you'll see a matching green, magenta and blue ring snap onto each band automatically — that's your confirmation the camera has locked on. No blue band? Just press Skip in the wizard and the game runs in two-marker mode (leaning will read as steering).
