⚠️ The #1 rule: don't reuse a color your bike or room already has. The tracker locks onto a color, so a yellow sticker on a yellow bike, or an orange/red sticker in front of warm wooden furniture, gets swamped by the bigger matching area and points at the wrong place. Red / green / blue are chosen to dodge skin, wood, and most bike frames — but glance at your own bike + background and swap any color that collides.

Where each band goes

This is roughly what your laptop camera sees, looking at the front of the bike.

Why these three?

The three hues sit far apart on the wheel, so the tracker tells them apart even in dim light or slight blur. And lime, pink and blue don't appear on skin, wood, or most walls, so the background won't create false blobs — unlike orange/yellow (wood, warm light, and many bike frames).

👍 Do

  • Use matte fluorescent tape — the brighter and more saturated, the better.
  • Make each band ~3–4 cm wide so it's a solid blob to the camera.
  • Put grips on the very ends of the bars, outside where the hands sit.
  • Put the blue band where the camera sees it and it doesn't turn with the bars.
  • Glance at your bike + background first and swap any color they share.

👎 Avoid

  • A marker color your bike frame already is (yellow sticker on a yellow bike, etc.).
  • Orange / warm colors in front of wooden furniture or warm lighting.
  • Two markers in the same family (e.g. light-blue grip + blue frame).
  • Glossy / metallic tape — glare blows out to white and loses its color.
  • Tiny or narrow strips, or a blue band on the parts that rotate.
💡 These are the app's default colors. During calibration you'll see a matching lime, pink and blue ring snap onto each band — that's your confirmation the camera locked on. Any three distinct, saturated colors also work: the wizard samples whatever you tap. No frame band? Press Skip for two-marker mode (leaning will read as steering).
